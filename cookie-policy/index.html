What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website saves to your browser so it can remember certain information between visits. Some last only for a session, while others persist for a set period defined by the site that created them. A first-party cookie comes from the site you visit; a third-party cookie comes from another domain delivering services like analytics or media. Similar technologies, such as local storage and pixels, may be used for the same purposes. We classify these tools by what they do rather than who provides them, because the function is what affects your experience. This is the model we use across vegashero, including experiences branded as vegas hero, to keep behavior consistent.

Measurement helps us see which pages are helpful and which need attention, and personalization keeps settings consistent across visits. Where allowed, vegashero may tailor content based on broad patterns rather than sensitive details. We avoid collecting more data than we need, and we retain it only for defined periods aligned with each category. Your Rights

Your browser gives you a range of controls over cookies and similar tools. You can delete stored data, block new cookies, or set prompts whenever a site wants to save one. In jurisdictions that require consent, non-essential categories are off until you choose otherwise. You may also withdraw consent later without losing access to core features that do not depend on it. These rights apply when you use products under vegas hero, and they are treated consistently across the vegashero network.

  1. Access information about categories of cookies in use.

  2. Update or withdraw consent for non-essential categories.

  3. Object to or limit certain processing tied to cookies where permitted by law.

  4. Request deletion of non-essential identifiers stored on your device via your browser.

  5. Request a portable copy of relevant logs where available and appropriate.

To exercise these options, start with your browser’s privacy controls, then review any on-site preferences you have set. Some settings take effect immediately, while others may require you to refresh the page. If a cookie is removed, features that relied on it may reset but will still operate in their basic form.
