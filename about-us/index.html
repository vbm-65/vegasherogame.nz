Information on the iGaming Platform Evaluation Methodology

Our evaluation starts with documents and ends with the experience of using the product under realistic constraints. We check legal permissions, product scope, and the footprint of payment partners before opening an account. Then we build test scenarios that match common user goals, like signing up, setting limits, and withdrawing a small amount. Each scenario has pass–fail criteria and a timing window to spot friction or delays. Findings are logged in a template so editors can audit the path that produced the score. Throughout this process we maintain a clean separation between reporting and opinion at vegashero, and we flag conflicts of interest across vegas hero.

  1. Verify licensing status with regulator records.

  2. Map the product: games, providers, RTP disclosures, and country restrictions.

  3. Test onboarding: KYC steps, deposit options, limits, and self-exclusion.

  4. Play and measure: stability, speed, fairness features, and crash handling.

  5. Withdraw and document: limits, fees, timelines, and support quality.

We convert raw notes into a weighted score only after two editors review the log. Weighting favors safety-critical factors over cosmetic polish, and we publish that weighting openly. If something cannot be verified, we say so and reduce its influence on the result. When a platform changes, the review gains a changelog entry so readers can track history. The aim is traceable, repeatable evaluation that a careful reader at vegas hero can understand without guessing at hidden rules.

Our verification process

Verification blends automation with human judgment. Scripts collect stable facts like license numbers and version histories, while reviewers validate context and spot edge cases. For sensitive items—identity checks, withdrawal limits, and affordability prompts—we run repeat tests at different hours to see if outcomes vary. Discrepancies are escalated to a second editor, and only resolved items affect the public score. We keep a private archive so any claim can be traced back to an artifact. That discipline is the backbone of vegashero, and it guides how we annotate tricky findings.
